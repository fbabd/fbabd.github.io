  • Predicting Antibody-Antigen Interactions with Structure-Aware LLMs: Insights from SARS-CoV-2 Variants
    Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling, 2025
    Faisal Bin Ashraf, Vinz Angelo Madrigal, Stefano Lonardi
    DOI: 10.1021/acs.jcim.5c00973

    Can protein language models better predict antibody binding and neutralization when they are taught to understand three-dimensional structure? In this work, I develop a structure-aware antibody language modeling framework, referred to as structure-aware ESM2, by augmenting pretrained ESM2 with a contact-map prediction objective. This enables joint learning of sequence and structural context while preserving general protein language understanding. By leveraging both experimental and ESMFold-predicted structures, the model learns antibody-specific representations that capture interaction-relevant geometry beyond sequence alone.

    The proposed approach achieves substantial performance gains, improving binding prediction AUC from 0.84 to 0.93 and neutralization prediction AUC from 0.88 to 0.95. Antibody-specific models consistently outperform pretrained and fine-tuned ESM2 baselines, as well as state-of-the-art methods including DeepAIR, AntiBERTa, AbMap, and A2Binder, while using a comparatively simple architecture. Notably, the results show that even approximate ESMFold structures provide meaningful structural signals that consistently improve downstream prediction accuracy and antibody–antigen embedding quality.

  • SPICE: Structural Protein Interaction Complex Evaluator
    Web-based research platform
    Faisal Bin Ashraf, Stefano Lonardi
    Webapp: https://spice.cs.ucr.edu/

    How can structural protein–protein interaction analysis be made accessible, reproducible, and scalable for comparative studies? SPICE is a publicly accessible, end-to-end web platform designed for comprehensive structural analysis of protein–protein complexes. It supports both single-complex and comparative variant analysis, enabling identification of intermolecular contacts, interface residues, energetic features, and backbone geometry.

    The platform facilitates mutation effect analysis by quantifying structural and physicochemical changes across related complexes, and integrates ML-ready backend data pipelines with interactive, publication-grade visualizations. SPICE is deployed as a production system and is actively used for exploratory and comparative studies of protein complexes.

  • A Large Language Model Guides the Affinity Maturation of Antibodies Generated by Combinatorial Optimization Algorithms
    Preprint (bioRxiv)
    Faisal Bin Ashraf, Karen Paco, Zihao Zhang, Christian J. Dávila Ojeda, Mariana P. Mendivil, Jordan A. Lay, Tristan Y. Yang, Fernando L. Barroso da Silva, Matthew H. Sazinsky, Animesh Ray, Stefano Lonardi
    DOI: 10.1101/2024.12.19.629473

    Can large language models be used not only to predict antibody binding affinity, but also to actively guide antibody optimization? In this work, I develop Ab-Affinity, a language model–based framework for predicting antibody–target binding affinity and integrating these predictions into an affinity maturation workflow. The model leverages sequence-level representations learned by large language models and is trained to accurately estimate binding strength against specific target peptides.

    Using the proposed framework, I demonstrate ML-guided antibody optimization by generating novel antibody variants with substantially improved binding, achieving up to ~160× increases in binding affinity compared with baseline experimental candidates. The approach further produces informative embedding representations and residue–residue contact map predictions that provide insight into structure–function relationships and guide sequence-level design decisions. Overall, this work shows how language model–driven affinity prediction can efficiently narrow the antibody sequence search space, reducing experimental screening effort and accelerating early-stage antibody development.
