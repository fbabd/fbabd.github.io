  • Predicting Antibody-Antigen Interactions with Structure-Aware LLMs: Insights from SARS-CoV-2 Variants
    Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling, 2025
    Faisal Bin Ashraf, Vinz Angelo Madrigal, Stefano Lonardi
    DOI

    Can protein language models better predict antibody binding and neutralization when they are taught to understand three-dimensional structure? In this work, I develop a structure-aware antibody language modeling framework, referred to as structure-aware ESM2, by augmenting pretrained ESM2 with a contact-map prediction objective. This enables joint learning of sequence and structural context while preserving general protein language understanding. By leveraging both experimental and ESMFold-predicted structures, the model learns antibody-specific representations that capture interaction-relevant geometry beyond sequence alone.

    The proposed approach achieves substantial performance gains, improving binding prediction AUC from 0.84 to 0.93 and neutralization prediction AUC from 0.88 to 0.95. Antibody-specific models consistently outperform pretrained and fine-tuned ESM2 baselines, as well as state-of-the-art methods including DeepAIR, AntiBERTa, AbMap, and A2Binder, while using a comparatively simple architecture. Notably, the results show that even approximate ESMFold structures provide meaningful structural signals that consistently improve downstream prediction accuracy and antibodyâ€“antigen embedding quality.
