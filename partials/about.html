About Me

Hi, I'm Faisal ...

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of California, Riverside, working under the supervision of Prof. Stefano Lonardi. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and computational biology, where I design intelligent solutions to decode the complexity of biological systems.

The rapid growth of high-throughput biotechnology has made it possible to generate massive amounts of biological data, from genomes to proteins to cellular interactions, far beyond what can be examined manually. My goal is to leverage modern AI techniques to make sense of these data and uncover new biological insights. My current work focuses on developing and fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) and generative AI systems to understand, evaluate, and design antibodies. By integrating multimodal biological data such as protein sequences, 3D structures, and molecular interactions, I aim to build AI models that can reason about biomolecular function and guide therapeutic design.

Broadly, I am interested in the intersection of deep learning and biology, including topics like protein language modeling, structure-based learning, graph neural networks, and generative modeling for molecular design. My work bridges machine intelligence with biological discovery, advancing computational methods that can ultimately transform how we study and engineer life.

Before joining UCR, I spent over six years teaching in the undergraduate program at Brac University, Bangladesh, where I taught courses such as Discrete Mathematics, Programming Languages, Database Systems, and Operating Systems. I'm originally from Chattogram, a beautiful coastal city in Bangladesh.
