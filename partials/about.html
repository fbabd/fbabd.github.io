About Me

Hi, I'm Faisal Bin Ashraf.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of California, Riverside, working under the supervision of Prof. Stefano Lonardi. My research focuses on developing machine learning methods for understanding and designing antibody–antigen interactions, at the intersection of artificial intelligence and computational biology.

My work centers on large language models for proteins and structure-aware learning, where I integrate sequence information with three-dimensional structures, molecular interactions, and graph-based representations. I design models that predict antibody binding, capture the effects of antigen mutations, and learn biologically meaningful representations that distinguish functional outcomes across variants. A key theme of my research is moving beyond black-box prediction toward models that explicitly encode structural and physicochemical context, enabling reliable interpretation and downstream design.

I have published in venues such as the Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling, PLOS ONE, and Heliyon, and my recent work demonstrates how structure-aware language models can reveal mutation-driven changes in antibody–antigen binding. In parallel, I develop practical research tools, including a web-based platform for comparative analysis of protein–protein complexes, supporting interface characterization, energetic analysis, and variant-level comparison.

Broadly, I am interested in protein language modeling, structure-based learning, graph neural networks, and generative models for molecular and antibody design. My long-term goal is to build AI systems that bridge machine intelligence with biological insight, enabling principled analysis and design of biomolecules for real-world biomedical and therapeutic applications.

Before joining UC Riverside, I spent over six years as a university lecturer in computer science at Brac University, Bangladesh, where I taught core undergraduate courses including Discrete Mathematics, Programming Languages, Database Systems, and Operating Systems. I am originally from Chattogram, a beautiful coastal city in Bangladesh.
